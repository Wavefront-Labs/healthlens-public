2. What We Collect

Information you provide

  • Health documents — PDFs, images, and other documents you upload for processing (pathology results, specialist letters, discharge summaries, imaging reports).
  • Medication & supplement information — product names, ingredients, dosage details, and photos you enter or photograph.
  • Personal notes — free-text notes and symptom logs you create.
  • Cycle data — menstrual cycle dates and symptoms, if you use cycle tracking.
  • Sign-in identity — your name and email address as provided by Apple Sign-In or Google Sign-In when you create an account.

Collected automatically

  • Device information — device type, OS version, and app version, used for crash reporting.
  • Subscription status — active/inactive/expired, managed by Apple App Store or Google Play. No payment card details.
  • Anonymised analytics — feature usage data, only with your consent during onboarding. No health content included.

What we do NOT collect

We do not collect your precise or approximate location. We do not share or sell your data to advertisers or data brokers.

3. How Your Health Data Is Processed

Your health data is stored locally on your device only. HealthLens does not maintain a server-side database of your health records, notes, medications, or summaries.

When you upload a document or request an AI summary:

  1. Your document is transmitted over encrypted HTTPS to our processing server in Sydney, Australia (DigitalOcean).
  2. The document content is forwarded to Anthropic (United States) for AI analysis using Claude models. This is a cross-border disclosure — see Section 4.
  3. The AI-generated result is returned to your device and stored locally.
  4. No health document content is retained on HealthLens servers after processing. The pipeline is stateless.

By using the App, you consent to this transient processing of your health information.

4. Cross-Border Disclosure & AI Processing

Document content is transmitted to Anthropic, Inc. (San Francisco, United States) for AI processing. This is a cross-border disclosure under the Australian Privacy Act and equivalent laws in our other launch markets.

  • Anthropic processes your document content solely to generate the AI analysis returned to your device.
  • Anthropic does not use API request content to train its models by default.
  • Anthropic retains API request logs for up to 30 days for safety monitoring, then deletes them.

If you do not wish your document content to be transmitted to Anthropic, do not use the document upload or AI summary features. Manual notes and cycle tracking operate entirely on-device with no cross-border disclosure.

5. AI Disclosure

HealthLens uses Anthropic’s Claude AI models to analyse documents and generate summaries. AI-generated content is labelled within the App. AI outputs may contain errors, omissions, or inaccuracies — they are for personal organisational purposes only and do not constitute medical advice.

HealthLens is not a Software as a Medical Device (SaMD) under the Australian Therapeutic Goods Act 1989 or equivalent legislation. It does not perform clinical decision support, diagnosis, or treatment recommendation functions.

6. Data Storage & Security

  • All health records, notes, medications, and summaries are stored in an encrypted database on your device.
  • All data transmitted between the App and the processing server is encrypted using TLS (HTTPS).
  • The HealthLens processing server does not persist your health data after processing.
  • Your device passcode and biometric lock protect your locally stored data.

Device backups (iCloud / Google Backup) may include HealthLens data under Apple’s and Google’s respective policies. Use Settings → Export to create your own portable backup.
